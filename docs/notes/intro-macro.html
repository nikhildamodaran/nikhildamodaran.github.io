From Growth to Trade

Reflection on Growth

  • In our theory of growth we argued two things:

    • Only growth in productivity can improve income per worker, permanently.
    • Transition towards steady state capital via capital accumulation induces temporary growth.

  • Adam Smith argued that the invisible hand maximizes welfare because:

    • it incentivizes specialisation of labour
    • it incentivizes free trade, expanding consumption possibilities

World Trade to GDP Ratio

Source: World Bank; Our World in Data

Trade = Exports + Imports. This ratio proxies how integrated the global economy is.

Reasons for Trade

  • Why do countries trade?

  • Differences in:

    • technologies and endowments
    • consumer tastes

  • Countries:

    • specialize in relatively efficient production
    • export those goods
    • import others

  • We study the Ricardian model (technology differences)

Production Possibilities Frontier

Setup

  • Two sectors: Manufacturing (\(m\)), Services (\(s\))

\[ Y_m = A_m L_m \qquad Y_s = A_s L_s \]

  • Labour constraint:

\[ L = L_m + L_s \]

PPF Derivation

\[ L_m = \frac{Y_m}{A_m}, \qquad L_s = \frac{Y_s}{A_s} \]

Substitute:

\[ L = \frac{Y_m}{A_m} + \frac{Y_s}{A_s} \]

Rearrange:

\[ Y_s = A_s L - \frac{A_s}{A_m} Y_m \]

Interpreting the PPF

\[ Y_s = A_s L - \frac{A_s}{A_m} Y_m \]

  • Intercept: \(A_s L\)
  • Slope (opportunity cost):

\[ \frac{dY_s}{dY_m} = -\frac{A_s}{A_m} \]

Numerical Example

  • \(L = 200\)
Services Manufactures
US 8 2
  • \(A_s = 8\), \(A_m = 2\)

\[ Y_s = 1600 - 4Y_m \]

  • Opportunity cost = 4 services

Consumption

  • Assume perfect complements:

\[ x = Y_s = Y_m \]

\[ x = A_s L - \frac{A_s}{A_m} x \Rightarrow x = \frac{A_m A_s}{A_m + A_s} L \]

Example:

\[ x = \frac{1600}{5} = 320 \]

Absolute and Comparative Advantage

Autarky vs Trade

  • Autarky: no trade
  • Free trade: countries exchange goods

Question: what drives trade?

Absolute Advantage

Services Manufactures
US 8 2
China 1 4
  • US: absolute advantage in services
  • China: absolute advantage in manufacturing

When One Country is Better at Everything

Services Manufactures
US 8 4
China 1 2
  • US has absolute advantage in both
  • Does trade still occur? → Yes

Comparative Advantage

  • What matters: opportunity cost

\[ \text{OC of } m = \frac{A_s}{A_m} \]

  • A country has comparative advantage if it produces at lower opportunity cost

Comparative Advantage Example

US:

\[ Y_s = 1600 - 2Y_m \]

China:

\[ Y_s = 800 - \frac{1}{2}Y_m \]

  • US: comparative advantage in services
  • China: comparative advantage in manufacturing

No Trade Outcome

US:

\[ x = 1600 - 2x \Rightarrow x = \frac{1600}{3} \]

China:

\[ x = 800 - \frac{1}{2}x \Rightarrow x = \frac{2}{3} \times 800 \]

  • Both consume \(533\frac{1}{3}\)

Specialisation

  • US → Services:

\[ Y_{s,US} = 1600 \]

  • China → Manufacturing:

\[ Y_{m,China} = 1600 \]

  • Global output rises from:

\[ 1066\frac{2}{3} \to 1600 \]

Trade Mechanism

  • Suppose:

\[ \frac{P_m}{P_s} = 1 \]

  • US:

    • produce → cost = 2 services
    • import → cost = 1 service

→ import manufactures

  • China:

    • produce → cost = 1/2 service
    • import → cost = 1 service

→ export manufactures

Gains from Trade

Consumption:

\[ x = 1600 - x \Rightarrow x = 800 \]

  • Both consume 800 (vs 533 before)
  • Both gain

Conditions for Trade

Trade occurs if price lies between opportunity costs:

\[ \frac{1}{2} < \frac{P_m}{P_s} < 2 \]

Learning Outcomes

  1. Derive and interpret the PPF
  2. Distinguish absolute vs comparative advantage
  3. Solve Ricardian model under autarky and trade

Key Formula

\[ Y_y = A_y L - \frac{A_y}{A_x} Y_x \]
